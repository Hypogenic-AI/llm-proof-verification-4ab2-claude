# Downloaded Papers

## CoT Reasoning and LLM Verification

1. **Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models** (2201.11903_chain_of_thought.pdf)
   - Authors: Wei, Wang, Schuurmans, Bosma, et al.
   - Year: 2022
   - arXiv: 2201.11903
   - Why relevant: Foundational CoT paper. Establishes emergence at scale, larger gains on harder problems.

2. **Let's Verify Step by Step** (2305.20050_lets_verify_step_by_step.pdf)
   - Authors: Lightman, Kosaraju, Burda, Edwards, et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2305.20050
   - Why relevant: PRM vs ORM comparison. Process supervision enables convergent verification.

3. **Self-Consistency Improves Chain of Thought Reasoning** (2203.11171_self_consistency.pdf)
   - Authors: Wang, Wei, Schuurmans, Le, et al.
   - Year: 2022
   - arXiv: 2203.11171
   - Why relevant: Majority voting over CoT paths as convergence mechanism.

4. **Tree of Thoughts: Deliberate Problem Solving with LLMs** (2305.10601_tree_of_thoughts.pdf)
   - Authors: Yao, Yu, Zhao, et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2305.10601
   - Why relevant: Structured search over reasoning paths; tree-like proof exploration.

5. **Let's think step by step (Zero-shot CoT)** (2205.11916_zero_shot_cot.pdf)
   - Authors: Kojima, Gu, Reid, Matsuo, Iwasawa
   - Year: 2022
   - arXiv: 2205.11916
   - Why relevant: Zero-shot variant of CoT; baseline comparison.
